# PR-TOP — Technical Brief for Presentation Video

## 1. Project Context

- **Brand:** PR-TOP
- **Meaning:** Practice on Top
- **Category:** AI-powered practice assistant for psychologists and therapists
- **Priority source of truth:** brand materials
- **Public-facing name:** PR-TOP only
- **Legacy/internal name:** PsyLink may exist in codebase, but must not appear in public-facing video materials

## 2. Goal

Create a concise, premium, therapist-first presentation video that introduces PR-TOP as a calm and credible AI support product for private practice.

The video should:

- explain what PR-TOP is in seconds
- show who it is for
- communicate the core value clearly
- build trust
- lead naturally to CTA

## 3. Core Communication Objective

After watching, the audience should understand:

- this product is built for psychologists and therapists
- it reduces routine and repeated communication
- it supports continuity between sessions
- it helps bring more clarity to day-to-day practice
- the therapist stays in control

## 4. Audience

Primary audience:

- private-practice psychologists
- psychotherapists
- counselors
- coaches with therapy-like client workflows
- small therapy centers and boutique practices

## 5. Brand Positioning

### Positioning statement

PR-TOP is an AI-powered practice support assistant for psychologists and therapists. It helps reduce routine, improve between-session continuity, and support better client communication while keeping the therapist in control.

### Short positioning

**AI support for psychologists who want their practice on top.**

### Brand promise

**Care for clients. Clarity for your practice.**

## 6. Emotional Outcome

The video should make the viewer feel:

- calm
- understood
- supported
- confident
- less overloaded

It should not feel like:

- startup hype
- aggressive sales content
- generic AI promo
- productivity cult messaging

## 7. Messaging Hierarchy

Use this order of meaning:

1. This is for therapists and psychologists.
2. It helps between sessions and in daily practice.
3. It reduces routine and supports continuity.
4. It keeps care human.
5. The therapist remains in control.

## 8. Approved Core Messages

- Keep Your Practice on Top
- Care for clients. Clarity for your practice.
- Built for therapists, not technologists.
- Less routine. More focus on care.
- Support between sessions.
- A calmer way to run your practice.
- Supportive AI — with the therapist in control.

## 9. Phrases to Avoid

- 10x your therapy business
- dominate your niche
- explode conversions
- magic AI
- replace therapists
- automate therapy
- AI runs your practice for you
- the future of mental health disruption

## 10. Visual Direction

The video must feel:

- calm
- structured
- modern
- minimal
- credible
- soft-tech
- premium without looking luxury-for-show

### Visual metaphors

- continuity
- support
- structure
- reflection
- trust
- guidance without pressure

### Avoid visually

- neon palettes
- cyberpunk AI styling
- aggressive dark hacker aesthetics
- cliché brain icons
- medical cross symbols
- generic SaaS blobs
- cartoon therapy imagery

## 11. Brand Color System

### Primary

- **Deep Ink** — `#163A43`
- **Therapy Teal** — `#1F8A83`

### Secondary

- **Soft Sage** — `#A8C9BE`
- **Mist** — `#F4F7F6`
- **Warm Sand** — `#ECE6DD`

### Neutrals

- **Heading Dark** — `#1C2328`
- **Body Gray** — `#5E6A71`
- **Border Light** — `#D9E2E0`
- **White** — `#FFFFFF`

### Optional AI accent

- **Lavender Signal** — `#7C7CF4`

Use rule:

- lead with deep ink + teal
- use lavender sparingly and only for subtle AI cues

## 12. Typography

- **Headlines:** Manrope or Plus Jakarta Sans
- **Body/UI:** Inter

Typography should feel:

- confident
- readable
- soft-authoritative
- spacious

## 13. Motion Direction

Use:

- smooth fades
- soft slides
- restrained zooms
- gentle parallax
- rounded UI transitions
- medium or slightly slow pacing

Avoid:

- glitch transitions
- hyper-fast edit rhythm
- strobe motion
- loud kinetic typography

## 14. Video Format

- **Master duration:** 60-90 seconds
- **Recommended hero cut:** 75 seconds
- **Cutdowns:** 30 seconds and 15 seconds
- **Formats:** 16:9, 1:1, 9:16
- **Primary language:** English
- **Optional adaptation:** Russian version based on same master structure

## 15. Recommended Narrative Structure

### Scene 1. Opening emotional frame

Objective:

- establish the invisible weight of private practice

Suggested line:

**Private practice is meaningful — and mentally heavy**

### Scene 2. The hidden workload

Objective:

- name the routine and context burden around care

Suggested ideas:

- notes
- follow-ups
- reminders
- client context
- repeated communication

### Scene 3. Brand solution

Objective:

- introduce PR-TOP as calm support, not AI spectacle

Suggested line:

**Care for clients. Clarity for your practice.**

### Scene 4. Value pillars

Objective:

- show the 3-4 strongest benefits

Suggested pillars:

- Between-session continuity
- Smarter communication
- Less routine admin
- Therapist-controlled support

### Scene 5. Trust and control

Objective:

- explicitly protect against the fear of losing therapeutic judgment

Suggested line:

**Supportive AI — with the therapist in control**

### Scene 6. Closing CTA

Objective:

- leave the viewer with the core brand promise and next action

Suggested line:

**Keep Your Practice on Top**

## 16. Safe Product Proof Points

Brand is the priority, but these product truths are safe to visualize as support:

- landing / therapist-facing product site
- therapist dashboard
- invite code workflow
- client detail workspace
- unified timeline
- diary entries: text, voice, video
- therapist notes
- client context
- exercises
- session summary / transcript states
- subscription tiers

## 17. Product Areas That Should Stay Secondary

- superadmin dashboard
- payment tables
- platform-wide metrics
- backend architecture
- developer or infrastructure details

## 21. Hard Brand Rules

Before approval, verify:

- PR-TOP is the only external brand name
- PsyLink does not appear publicly
- tone stays calm and professional
- therapist-first framing is stronger than AI-first framing
- there is an explicit control/trust moment
- color and typography follow the brand system
- the product is presented as support, not replacement

## 22. Success Criteria

The video is successful if:

- a therapist immediately recognizes the product is for them
- the message feels credible and brand-consistent
- the video feels calm and premium
- the product proof points support the story without taking over
- the CTA feels natural rather than salesy
