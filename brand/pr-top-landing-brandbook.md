# PR-TOP — Landing Brand Book

## Version

---

## 1. Quick review of the current first landing

Based on the screenshot, the current landing already has a solid structure:

### What works
- Clean, modern SaaS-style layout
- Calm visual tone that fits psychology better than aggressive startup aesthetics
- Clear hero with headline + supporting text + CTA
- Trustworthy whitespace and simple navigation
- Good direction for a therapist-facing product rather than a mass-market consumer app

### What feels weak or unfinished
- The current brand in the screenshot still reads more like **PsyLink** than a distinct product story
- The illustration is generic SaaS art; it does not yet communicate psychological care, trust, or therapist workflow
- The headline is understandable, but still a bit functional and not yet emotionally strong
- The CTA area is generic; it does not anchor the core promise strongly enough
- There is not yet a clear bridge between **clinical sensitivity** and **practical value**

### Strategic conclusion
The landing direction is good, but it needs a stronger brand layer:
- clearer positioning
- more emotionally precise language
- a visual system that feels calm, credible, and professional
- messaging aimed at **private-practice psychologists / therapists**, not at generic “users”

## 2. Brand foundation

## Brand name
**PR-TOP**

## Full meaning
**Practice on Top**

## Core idea
PR-TOP helps psychologists keep their practice on top: organized, responsive, calm, and professionally consistent.

## Positioning statement
PR-TOP is an AI-powered practice support assistant for psychologists and therapists. It helps reduce routine, improve between-session continuity, and support better client communication while keeping the therapist in control.

## Short positioning
**AI support for psychologists who want their practice on top.**

---

## 3. Audience

### Primary audience
- Private-practice psychologists
- Therapists
- Counselors
- Coaches with a therapy-like client workflow
- Small therapy centers and boutique practices

### Their pain points
- Too much admin and repeated client communication
- Difficulty maintaining continuity between sessions
- Mental overload from notes, follow-ups, reminders, and client context
- Fear of becoming robotic or impersonal when using technology
- Need for structure without losing therapeutic sensitivity

### What they want
- More clarity
- Less routine
- Better continuity between sessions
- Stronger organization
- A calmer workday
- Technology that supports, not replaces, the therapist

---

## 4. Brand promise

### Functional promise
PR-TOP helps psychologists reduce administrative friction and support client work between sessions.

### Emotional promise
PR-TOP helps therapists feel more organized, more supported, and less alone in the daily weight of practice management.

### Brand promise line
**Care for clients. Clarity for your practice.**

---

## 5. Brand values

- **Clarity** — reduce noise, make decisions easier
- **Care** — respect the sensitivity of therapist-client work
- **Professionalism** — serious, trustworthy, clinically aware
- **Calm** — never loud, manipulative, or “growth-hacky” in tone
- **Support** — the therapist remains in charge; the product assists
- **Practicality** — real workflow help, not vague inspiration

---

## 6. Brand personality

PR-TOP should feel like:
- calm
- intelligent
- grounded
- trustworthy
- respectful
- structured
- modern without being flashy

### Archetype mix
- **Caregiver** — supportive, steady, humane
- **Sage** — clear, wise, structured

### Avoid sounding like
- hype marketing
- bro-SaaS
- productivity cult language
- “replace therapists with AI” nonsense
- cold enterprise software

---

## 7. Tone of voice

### Tone rules
- Clear and human
- Warm, but restrained
- Professional, not stiff
- Confident, not overclaiming
- Calm, not salesy

### Good examples
- “Built for therapists, not technologists.”
- “Less routine. More focus on care.”
- “Support between sessions, with the therapist in control.”
- “A calmer way to run your practice.”

### Avoid
- “10x your therapy business”
- “Dominate your niche”
- “Explode conversions”
- “Replace admin forever with magic AI”

---

## 8. Visual direction

### Design goal
The landing should look like a modern health-tech / therapist-tech brand:
- calm
- minimal
- credible
- soft-edged
- structured
- premium but not luxury-for-show

### Visual principles
- Plenty of whitespace
- Rounded UI and cards
- Soft contrast
- Measured typography
- Few but confident accent colors
- Illustration and UI mockups should reflect therapist workflow, not generic dashboards only

### Best visual metaphors
- continuity
- support
- reflection
- structure
- trust
- thoughtful flow
- guidance without pressure

### Avoid visually
- neon cyberpunk palettes
- dark aggressive hacker themes
- overly corporate hospital aesthetics
- random startup blobs with no meaning
- cartoon therapy imagery that feels unserious

---

## 9. Color system

Recommended palette:

### Primary colors
- **Deep Ink** — `#163A43`
  - Primary text, headlines, trust, structure
- **Therapy Teal** — `#1F8A83`
  - Primary brand accent, buttons, highlights

### Secondary colors
- **Soft Sage** — `#A8C9BE`
  - Calm, supportive accents, backgrounds, feature chips
- **Mist** — `#F4F7F6`
  - Main section backgrounds
- **Warm Sand** — `#ECE6DD`
  - Human warmth for supporting surfaces

### Neutral system
- **Heading Dark** — `#1C2328`
- **Body Gray** — `#5E6A71`
- **Border Light** — `#D9E2E0`
- **White** — `#FFFFFF`

### Optional accent
- **Lavender Signal** — `#7C7CF4`
  - Use sparingly for chat/AI visual cues only

### Color usage rule
The brand should be led by **teal + deep ink**, with lavender only as a subtle product/AI accent.

---

## 10. Typography

### Headline font direction
Use a clean sans-serif with strong readability and soft authority.

Recommended:
- **Inter**
- **Manrope**
- **Plus Jakarta Sans**
- **Sora** (only if used carefully)

### Recommended pairing
- **Headlines:** Manrope or Plus Jakarta Sans
- **Body/UI:** Inter

### Typography feel
- Headlines: bold, clean, calm confidence
- Body: readable, spacious, not dense
- Avoid overly geometric or ultra-tech fonts

---

## 11. Logo direction

### Logo idea
Wordmark-first brand.

**PR-TOP** should be the hero, with a clean typographic treatment.

### Possible logo directions
1. **Wordmark only**
   - PR-TOP in deep ink
   - subtle teal accent on hyphen or “TOP”

2. **Wordmark + signal glyph**
   - small icon suggesting connection, continuity, or support
   - e.g. soft linked shapes, minimal upward line, rounded dialogue mark

3. **Wordmark + practice mark**
   - a minimal top-line / horizon / supporting arc to express “on top” without cliché arrows

### Avoid in logo
- brain icons
- cliché chat bubbles
- medical cross
- lotus flower unless extremely abstract
- stock “growth arrow” visuals

- therapist workflow UI mockup
